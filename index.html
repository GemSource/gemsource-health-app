Outreach Messaging

Copy-paste ready. Natural and curiosity-driven — never salesy.

First DM / Connection Note
Tone: Cold → Curious
Hey [Name], quick one — I'm working with a couple of dental clinics in NZ that are hiring right now. Some are open to relocating dentists from Australia. Would you ever consider a move for the right opportunity?
↳Low pressure, not salesy, opens a curiosity loop — gets replies.
Follow-Up (No Reply After 1–2 Days)
Tone: Soft Nudge
Out of curiosity — is NZ something you'd ever consider down the track, or not really on your radar?
↳Binary question. Easy to answer. Almost always gets a response.
They Replied 'Maybe / Depends'
Tone: Unlock Mode
Nice — that's usually how it starts. What would need to be right for you to actually consider a move?
↳This question is gold. It unlocks their real motivations and tells you exactly how to close them.
Voice Note Script
Tone: Power Move
Hey mate — easier to explain via voice. The clinics we're working with in NZ are actively hiring and open to relocating dentists. Thought you could be a strong fit based on your background…
↳Builds trust fast, differentiates instantly, feels human. Most recruiters won't do this — that's why it works.
Mistakes to Avoid
✕Don't send long messages
✕Don't pitch roles too early
✕Don't over-qualify cold leads
✕Don't wait for perfect candidates

Qualification Script

You're exploring, not interrogating. Keep it casual. Run this in order.

01
Motivation
"What's got you even slightly open to something new?"
Listen for push factors — burnout, pay, culture, growth ceiling.
02
Current Situation
"How are you finding your current clinic?"
Let them vent. Pain = opportunity.
03
Salary / Earnings
"Out of curiosity, what are you currently earning roughly?"
Never skip this. Misaligned salary = wasted time for everyone.
04
Expectations
"What would a move need to look like financially?"
Sets your brief benchmark before you pitch anything.
05
Timing
"If the right role came up, when would you realistically move?"
Separates warm leads from future pipeline.
06
Risk Check ⚠
"What would hold you back from actually making the move?"
THIS is where placements are won or lost. Uncover it now, not at offer stage.

Sourcing Engine

Part One of your GemSource Health NZ Playbook — your daily revenue-generating machine.

Core Recruitment Principle

"Do not just find dentists. Find movable dentists. The commercial edge is identifying clinicians who are open to change, open to regional work, open to relocation, and looking for growth, mentorship, or a better long-term fit."

Candidate Market Focus
Primary Markets
→ NZ-based dentists for NZ clinics
→ AU-based dentists open to relocating to NZ
Target Candidate Types
→ Fresh graduates (BDS or DDS qualified)
→ Early-career dentists (0–3 years post-grad)
→ Junior associates seeking better mentorship, culture, or location
Channel Priority
Tier 1
Clinic-Driven Sourcing · LinkedIn Sourcing
Highest immediate commercial value — spend most of your daily time here
Tier 2
Job Boards · Networks / Communities / Referrals
Strong support channels
Tier 3
Universities / Graduate Pipeline
Long-term pipeline — 6–18 month horizon
Strong Fit Signals
Limited progression in current role
Previously relocated — proven mobility
Wants more autonomy or mentorship
Interested in NZ long-term
Regional background or lifestyle focus
Asks thoughtful questions about culture
Red Flags
Completely city-locked, zero flexibility
Only wants the 'perfect role'
Purely money-driven
Vague on motivations or slow to engage
No thought around DCNZ registration
Escaping a bad situation — not a genuine move
Engine 1 — Clinic-Driven Sourcing

Best immediate revenue engine — real vacancy, real brief, real urgency.

Step-by-Step
01  Confirm the role properly — level, AU candidates considered, relocation support, mentorship, salary, timeline
02  Write an internal targeting note before outreach begins
03  Build target list — NZ juniors, AU open-to-relocation, recent grads, GSE bench
04  Lead outreach with candidate's interests — not the full role pitch
05  Screen for mobility — location flexibility, support needed, timing, real motivations
06  Only shortlist genuinely interested, movable, credible, aligned candidates
Common Mistakes
✕ Sourcing before the brief is fully clear
✕ Assuming all junior dentists will relocate
✕ Overselling the opportunity too early
✕ Chasing candidates who aren't emotionally ready to move
Engine 2 — LinkedIn Sourcing

Precision targeting tool — not a spam tool. Best for 1–3 year dentists and AU-to-NZ sourcing.

What to Look For
→ 12–24 months in current role — approaching the natural 'restless' window
→ Movement history — previously relocated or changed cities
→ Signs of being under-mentored or under-challenged
→ Interest in growth, development, or regional work in their profile
Candidate Tiers
A-TIERRight level, looks movable — reach out now
B-TIERPossibly suitable, mobility unclear — warm touch, long-term nurture
C-TIERToo senior, city-locked, or weak fit — do not prioritise
Follow-Up Sequence
1. Connection request
2. First follow-up message (1–2 days after acceptance)
3. Final follow-up — if still no reply, move on
4. If they engage — progress to phone screen quickly, don't let warm leads go cold
Engine 3 — University / Graduate Pipeline

Not your fastest engine — a long-term competitive advantage. Builds 6–18 month pipelines.

Target Schools
→ University of Otago — Faculty of Dentistry (NZ)
→ University of Melbourne Dental School
→ University of Sydney — Faculty of Dentistry
→ Griffith University, James Cook University, Charles Sturt University
How to Position Yourself
Don't lead with "I'm a recruiter with jobs." Lead with: helping graduates understand career pathways, choose the right first clinic, and navigate AU vs NZ options.
Engine 4 — Job Boards & Market Activity

Use ads as market intelligence — not just a candidate source.

Boards to Track
→ Seek NZ — primary NZ dental board
→ Trade Me Jobs — NZ general board with healthcare listings
→ Seek AU — for sourcing AU dentists open to NZ
What to Look For in Ads
→ Repeat advertisers — clinics re-posting the same role signal retention issues
→ Regional roles — high value, harder to fill, good sourcing opportunity
→ Cross-reference on LinkedIn — check current dentists and recent departures at that clinic
Engine 5 — Networks, Communities & Referrals

Candidates trust peers more than cold recruiters. Powerful when worked consistently.

Communities to Engage
→ NZ Dental Association (NZDA)
→ Australian Dental Association (ADA)
→ Dental student societies at target universities
→ Facebook groups for NZ dentists and dental graduates
→ Koira Consulting NZ — partnership referral pipeline
Rule: Observe before engaging. Add value before asking for anything. Once trust exists, ask: "Who else in your cohort is open to regional work or considering NZ?"

Critical Mistakes to Avoid
✕Chasing everyone — volume without qualification wastes time and damages reputation
✕Focusing on qualifications only — a qualified dentist who won't move is not a candidate
✕Taking vague clinic briefs to market — no brief, no sourcing
✕Overselling relocation — it's a major life decision, treat it seriously
✕Being reactive — build pipeline every week, not only when a brief lands
✕Poor GSE discipline — unlogged pipeline is invisible pipeline
✕Ignoring nurture candidates — many placements come from candidates nurtured over weeks
